04.1 — Documentation Preview

Engineering highlights

Custom Cursor System
JavaScript · Snippet
// LazyDog Studios — Custom Cursor with Magnetic Labels
// Three-layer cursor: instant dot, eased ring, contextual label

const dot = document.querySelector('.cursor-dot');
const ring = document.querySelector('.cursor-ring');
let mouseX = 0, mouseY = 0, ringX = 0, ringY = 0;

document.addEventListener('mousemove', e => {
  mouseX = e.clientX;
  mouseY = e.clientY;
  dot.style.left = mouseX + 'px';
  dot.style.top = mouseY + 'px';
});

// Eased ring follow — creates magnetic tension
function animateRing() {
  ringX += (mouseX - ringX) * 0.15;
  ringY += (mouseY - ringY) * 0.15;
  ring.style.left = ringX + 'px';
  ring.style.top = ringY + 'px';
  requestAnimationFrame(animateRing);
}

09.1 — Our Process

Five phases, performed with care

01

Discovery

We begin with conversation — about your studio, your audience, your ambitions. We research your field, study your competitors, and develop a brief that is discovered, not assumed. This phase typically takes one to two weeks.
02

Direction

We present three distinct creative directions — each with its own visual language, typographic system, and motion philosophy. You choose one. We refine it together until the direction feels inevitable. Two weeks.
03

Design

Every page, every component, every interaction is designed in detail. We work in Figma and in code simultaneously — because some decisions can only be made in the medium. Three to four weeks, depending on scope.
04

Development

We build in HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript — or in the framework of your choice. Clean, commented, accessible code. No build step unless your team requires one. Two to four weeks.
05

Launch & Care

We launch together. Then we stay — for documentation, for training, for the inevitable iterations. Every custom project includes 90 days of post-launch support at no additional cost.

11 — About LazyDog Studios

A small collective, a large catalogue.

LazyDog Studios was founded in 2019 in Brooklyn, New York — a small collective of designers and engineers who had grown tired of the template marketplace and its race to the bottom.

We make digital artifacts — WebKits, UI systems, decks, and branding assets — designed with the same care a studio would bring to a client commission. Then we sell them to other studios, who adapt them for their own clients.

Five years later, we are still small. Eight people. One studio in Brooklyn. A catalogue of 128 artifacts. And a commitment to making the best digital design products in the world.

13.1 — License FAQ

Common questions

Can I use a WebKit for client projects?
Yes. The Studio License allows you to use any artifact on unlimited commercial and client projects. You can modify, white-label, and resell the final website to your client. You cannot resell the artifact itself.
Do I need to attribute LazyDog Studios?
No. Attribution is appreciated but never required. Your clients do not need to know you used our WebKit — though many tell us they do mention it, which we appreciate.
What does "free lifetime updates" mean?
When you purchase an artifact, you receive all future updates to that artifact at no additional cost. When we release Meridian v2, you get it free. When we release v3, same. Forever.
Can I get a refund?
Yes. We offer a 14-day no-questions-asked refund policy. If an artifact doesn't work for you, email us within 14 days of purchase and we'll refund you in full.
14 — Frequently Asked Questions

Questions, answered.

Everything you might want to know about our artifacts, our licenses, our process, and our studio. If your question isn't here, write to us.

What exactly do I get when I purchase a WebKit?
You receive a downloadable .zip containing the full HTML/CSS/JS source code, all image assets (or placeholders with sourcing notes), the Google Fonts and Font Awesome integration, a README file, and the complete design dossier — a multi-page document covering every design decision, the engineering architecture, and the component reusability guide.
Are the WebKits responsive?
Yes. Every WebKit includes responsive breakpoints at 1024px and 640px, with careful attention to typographic scaling, grid behavior, and touch interactions. The custom cursor automatically disables on touch devices.
Do I need to know how to code?
For basic customization (changing text, images, colors), minimal HTML/CSS knowledge is sufficient. For deeper customization, we recommend comfort with HTML, CSS, and basic JavaScript. If you'd rather we handle the customization, our WebKit Customization service starts at $499.
Can I use a WebKit for more than one project?
Yes. The Studio License allows unlimited commercial projects. You can use the same WebKit for ten different clients if you wish — though we recommend customizing each one to avoid repetition.
What frameworks do you use?
Our WebKits are built in vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no framework dependencies. This ensures maximum portability and longevity. You can integrate them into any framework (React, Vue, Astro, Next.js) yourself, or commission us to do it for you.
How often are artifacts updated?
We update artifacts when we find bugs, when browser standards change, or when we feel the design can be improved. Major updates are announced in our journal. All updates are free for life.
Do you offer custom design services?
Yes. We take on a limited number of custom commissions each quarter — websites, UI systems, brand identities. See our Custom Design page for details, or contact us to start a conversation.
Who is behind LazyDog Studios?
Eight people, based in Brooklyn, New York. We were founded in 2019 by Marcus Reid and Sasha Chen. We are designers and engineers — no project managers, no marketing department. Read more on our About page.
